I'm reaching out from the Mailspring team. Your MakeUseOf piece on the best Linux email clients is a go-to resource, and we appreciate Mailspring being part of it.

We've shipped a wave of updates over the last few months that significantly change what Mailspring offers, and I wanted to make sure you had them on your radar for any future updates to the article:

**Calendar is here:** Full CalDAV calendar with month/week/day/agenda views, event creation and editing, drag-to-reschedule, recurring events, and search. All synced to the server. This closes the biggest gap reviewers have pointed to.

**Linux platform work:**
- Native Wayland support (Electron 39)
- ARM64/aarch64 builds
- Official AUR package for Arch
- System tray fixes across GNOME, Unity, Ubuntu 25
- Thin window framing + hamburger menu as the default on Linux
- Better Fedora dependency handling
- IPv6 SMTP support

**Usability improvements:**
- Built-in grammar checking (LanguageTool)
- One-click unsubscribe (RFC 2369/8058)
- Full accessibility overhaul (ARIA, focus traps, semantic landmarks)
- VCard import/export
- Thread context menu reorganized to prevent accidental destructive actions

Your article mentions Mailspring as a Nylas Mail fork — which is accurate history, but we've moved well beyond that at this point. The codebase, sync engine, and feature set are substantially different from what Nylas was.
